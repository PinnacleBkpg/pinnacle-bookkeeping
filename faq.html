About Bookkeeping
A bookkeeper records, organizes, and reconciles all the financial transactions in your business. That means every purchase, every sale, every payment, and every deposit gets recorded in the correct account, in the correct period, with the correct category. At the end of each month, you get clean financial statements — Profit & Loss, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow — that actually tell you how the business is doing. A good bookkeeper is also watching for errors, missed expenses, and compliance deadlines. Think of us as your financial back office.
A bookkeeper handles the day-to-day and month-to-month recording of transactions, reconciliations, and financial statements. An accountant typically works on higher-level analysis, tax strategy, corporate returns (T2), and year-end work. In practice: your bookkeeper keeps the records clean all year so your accountant can do their job quickly and accurately at year-end — saving you money on accounting fees. Many clients work with both.
You can absolutely do your own bookkeeping — and many business owners do, at first. But there are real costs: the time it takes (15–20 hours/month is typical), the errors that accumulate, the deductions that get missed, and the CRA penalties that can come from late or inaccurate filings. Most business owners find that outsourcing bookkeeping pays for itself in time saved and mistakes avoided. The question isn't whether you can — it's whether you should.
The honest answer: earlier than most people do. The sweet spot is usually when your business has registered for HST (meaning you're doing at least $30,000 in revenue), when you're spending more than a few hours per week on your books, or when you have employees. If tax season feels like a scramble every year — that's also a clear sign. The longer you wait, the more catch-up work is required.

CRA & Compliance

We'll ensure the amounts are remitted to the CRA on schedule — regular remitters, accelerated remitters, whatever your frequency. Yes — HST filing is one of our core compliance services. We prepare your HST return, calculate the net tax owing (or refund), account for your Input Tax Credits (ITCs), and file with the CRA on time. We work with monthly, quarterly, and annual filers. We'll also help you understand what's happened in each period so there are no surprises when the filing goes in.
In Canada, HST registration is mandatory once your business revenue exceeds $30,000 in a single calendar quarter or in four consecutive calendar quarters. If you're not yet registered but approaching that threshold, we'll flag it. We can assist with the registration process and advise on whether voluntary early registration makes sense for your business.
