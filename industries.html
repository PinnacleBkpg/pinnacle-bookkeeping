Restaurants & Food Service

The Most Complex Books in Small Business

Restaurants operate with thin margins, high transaction volumes, and an unusually complex mix of payroll, inventory, tips, and HST rules. Most bookkeepers don't fully understand food service. We do.

The Challenge

Daily POS sales need to be reconciled against cash and card deposits. Tip income must be tracked and reported. Food costs need to be separated from other expenses. And then there's the HST — different rates depending on whether food is prepared, packaged, or delivered.

Our Solution

We integrate directly with your POS system (Square, Toast, Lightspeed), reconcile daily sales, track food and beverage costs as separate line items, and handle the full payroll cycle including tip allocation. Your books reflect the real financial health of your restaurant — not just a pile of receipts.

What Restaurant Owners Get

  • Daily or weekly sales reconciliation against POS and bank deposits
  • Food cost tracking and gross margin analysis
  • Split-shift and variable-hour payroll processing
  • Tip tracking, pooling, and T4 tip reporting
  • Vendor and food supplier invoice management
  • HST filing with correct treatment of food, beverages, and catering

    Contractors & Trades

    Job Costing, Holdbacks, and the CRA — All Handled

    Contractors face a unique financial picture: irregular income, project-based expenses, subcontractors to pay, holdbacks to track, and equipment costs that need to be capitalized properly. It's a lot. We've built our processes specifically for trades businesses.

    The Challenge

    Revenue comes in uneven chunks. Costs are spread across jobs and are difficult to track without a proper system. Subcontractor payments require T4As at year-end. Holdbacks sit on the Balance Sheet until released. Miss any of this and your financials are meaningless.

    Our Solution

    We set up job costing in QuickBooks Online so every dollar in and out is tied to a project. We track holdbacks, manage subcontractor payments, and prepare T4As. You can see which jobs made money and which ones didn't — in real time.

    What Contractors Get

  • Job costing and project profitability reporting per contract
  • Progress billing and holdback release tracking
  • Subcontractor payment tracking and T4A preparation
  • Equipment, vehicle, and tool expense management
  • WSIB filing and compliance
  • HST on materials vs. labour — properly handled

    Service Businesses

    Clean Monthly Numbers for Businesses That Run on Relationships

    Service businesses — cleaners, landscapers, IT firms, marketing agencies, consultants — often have clean, simple operations but messy books. The work gets done. The invoicing gets forgotten. The books pile up. We fix that.

    The Challenge

    Service businesses often have outstanding invoices sitting in AR for months. They mix business and personal expenses. They hire contractors and don't track T4A obligations. And at year-end, nobody knows what the margin was.

    Our Solution

  • Contractor vs. employee classification and T4A management

    Retail Stores

    High-Volume Sales, Inventory, and Tight Margins — We Speak Retail

    Retail bookkeeping means high transaction volumes, POS reconciliations across multiple payment types, inventory tracking, and cost of goods sold. It's detailed work that requires a systematic approach.

    The Challenge

    Retail owners deal with hundreds or thousands of transactions per month. POS deposits don't always match bank deposits cleanly. Inventory shrinkage goes untracked. COGS gets mixed with operating expenses. And by the time tax season arrives, the margin picture is unclear.

    Our Solution

    We connect directly to your POS system, reconcile daily sales by payment type, and ensure your inventory and COGS are tracked separately from operating expenses. Your gross margin is always visible — so you know whether your pricing is working.

  • POS integration with QuickBooks Online (Square, Shopify, Lightspeed)
  • Daily or weekly sales reconciliation by payment type
  • Inventory tracking and cost of goods sold (COGS) separation
  • Shrinkage, returns, and write-off recording
  • Gross margin reporting by product category
  • HST on taxable vs. exempt items properly tracked

    Professional Services

    Your Time Is Billable. Bookkeeping Shouldn't Be.

    Lawyers, consultants, healthcare practitioners, architects, engineers, and other professionals often run incorporated businesses with unique financial structures. We understand professional corporations, partnership draws, and the specific compliance obligations of knowledge-based firms.

    The Challenge

    Professional service firms often have complex billing structures — retainers, hourly, project-based. Partners take draws in different ways. Personal and corporate expenses blur together. And the billable hour is too valuable to spend on reconciling accounts.

    Our Solution

    We set up clean billing and AR workflows, manage partner or owner draws correctly, and ensure your corporate and personal expenses are cleanly separated. Every month you get financials that actually reflect the health of the practice.

  • Retainer, project, and hourly invoice tracking and AR management
  • Professional expense categorization (memberships, insurance, software)
  • Owner/partner draw and salary vs. dividend structure management
  • Corporate and personal expense separation
  • HST on professional services — billing, ITCs, and quarterly filing
  • Coordination with your external accountant for T2 corporate returns
